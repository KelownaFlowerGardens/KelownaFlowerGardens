Bridal Bouquets

The history, beauty, and symbolism of wedding florals.

Caroline, Princess of Monaco

Married June 1978, carried tiny white orchids.

Grace Kelly, Princess of Monaco

In 1956, Grace Kelly married Prince Ranier Grimaldi of Monaco, carrying Lilies-of-the-Valley. Her matrons carried Tea Roses, and the flower girls White Daisies.

Victoria, Queen of the United Kingdom

Married Prince Albert in 1840 in the Royal Chapel at St. James Palace. She carried a tiny bouquet of Snowdrops, and wore a wreath crown, of white porcelain Orange Blossoms designed specifically for this event.

Silvia Renate Sommerlath, Queen of Sweden1

Married to King Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden, Silvia chose Orchids and Jasmine, with medium-sized cascading Posy, arranged among Stephanotis, Lily of the Valley and Sprigs of Myrtle.

Victoria, Princess of Sweden

Victoria's bouquet consisted of the traditional flowers seen in Royal Weddings with more flowers adding to the cascading effect. Azaleas, Bleeding Heart, Clematis, Cosmos, Dicentra Formosa, Gardenias, Lilies, Lily of the Valley, Marbacka Pelargonium, Orchids, Peonies, Roses, Sweet Peas, Waxflower and Myrtle of the same garden as her mothers bouquet.

Muriel Vanderbilt

The American Society wedding at Newport, to Frederick Cameron Church Jr. had the veil of Marie Antoinette purchased for the event. She carried a bouquet of Orchids and Gardenias.
